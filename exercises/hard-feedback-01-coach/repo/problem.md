# Receiving Hard Feedback

The question you are building an answer for:

> Tell me about a time you received difficult feedback. How did you respond, and what changed?

The story is graded on what you did with the feedback, not on how gracefully you sat through it. A defensive first reaction is fine to admit -- what matters is the concrete behavior change afterward.

Work through it with your coach one STAR section at a time --
Situation, Task, Action, Result, then Reflection -- writing each into
`solution.md` as you go. The coach reads what you wrote and pushes for
specifics; expect to rewrite sections until they're concrete.

## What good looks like

By the end you should have: one real, named situation with stakes;
your own role stated plainly; actions in "I" statements with the
decisions visible; results with evidence; and a reflection that names
an actual change in how you work.

Submit with M-q when the story stands on its own.
