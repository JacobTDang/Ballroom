# Grading Rubric — Disagreement With a Teammate

Grade each dimension: strong / adequate / missing. A passing answer is
adequate-or-better on every dimension.

## 1. Situation specificity

- A disagreement with real technical or product stakes -- named project, named decision, what each side wanted.
- A listener could picture it: real project, real people, real time
  pressure -- not a composite or hypothetical.

## 2. Task & stakes

- Your stake in the disagreement: what you believed and what outcome you were responsible for.
- Why it mattered is stated -- what would have happened otherwise.

## 3. Action depth & ownership

- How you argued it: evidence over volume, understanding their position well enough to state it fairly, escalating or conceding deliberately.
- Told in "I" statements: the candidate's own decisions and words, not
  "we" hiding the team's work.

## 4. Result evidence

- How it resolved -- including disagree-and-commit if you lost -- and what the working relationship looked like after.
- Concrete evidence, not adjectives: numbers, shipped outcomes, or
  observable reactions from the people involved.

## 5. Reflection

- What the disagreement taught you about how you argue, not just what the right answer was.
- The lesson changed something checkable about how they work since.
