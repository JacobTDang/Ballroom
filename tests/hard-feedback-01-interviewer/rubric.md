# Grading Rubric — Receiving Hard Feedback

Grade each dimension: strong / adequate / missing. A passing answer is
adequate-or-better on every dimension.

## 1. Situation specificity

- Feedback that was hard to hear -- who gave it, what they actually said, and the context.
- A listener could picture it: real project, real people, real time
  pressure -- not a composite or hypothetical.

## 2. Task & stakes

- What was at stake in taking it seriously (a project, a relationship, your growth).
- Why it mattered is stated -- what would have happened otherwise.

## 3. Action depth & ownership

- How you processed it: clarifying instead of defending, testing whether it was true, deciding what to change.
- Told in "I" statements: the candidate's own decisions and words, not
  "we" hiding the team's work.

## 4. Result evidence

- The visible change, and evidence someone else noticed (follow-up feedback, changed outcomes).
- Concrete evidence, not adjectives: numbers, shipped outcomes, or
  observable reactions from the people involved.

## 5. Reflection

- What it taught you about how you receive feedback -- honestly, including what's still hard.
- The lesson changed something checkable about how they work since.
